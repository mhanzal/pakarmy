About Pakistan Army

Recent Operations

Operation Sindoor: India’s Unjustified Escalation

On May 7, 2025, India launched airstrikes in Pakistan and Pakistan-administered Kashmir under the codename Operation Sindoor, claiming to target terrorist camps. The strikes followed an April 22 attack in Pahalgam, which India blamed on the group TRF — a claim Pakistan denies. Pakistan condemned the strikes as baseless and a violation of its sovereignty, calling them an attempt by India to divert attention from internal issues. Critics argue that India presented no solid evidence and acted recklessly, risking further instability in the region.

1947–48 (First Kashmir War)

The First Kashmir War began in October 1947 between India and Pakistan after tribal forces from Pakistan entered Jammu and Kashmir. To get Indian military help, the Maharaja signed the Instrument of Accession, joining India—a move Pakistan opposed. The war lasted until a UN ceasefire in January 1949, which left Kashmir divided: India kept about two-thirds, and Pakistan took one-third. The conflict started the ongoing Kashmir dispute between the two nations.
